# 7. Text & Graph Embeddings

Embeddings are ways to convert text, words, or graph nodes into numerical vectors so machines can understand relationships.

---

## What are Embeddings?

Embeddings map data into a continuous vector space where similar items are closer together.

Example:
- “king” and “queen” → close vectors  
- “cat” and “dog” → similar vectors  

---

## Word Embeddings

Word embeddings represent words as vectors.

Popular models:
- Word2Vec
- GloVe
- FastText

---

## Word2Vec Idea

Words appearing in similar contexts have similar meanings.

Example:
- “doctor” ↔ “nurse”
- “car” ↔ “vehicle”

---

## Simple Vector Representation

    king → [0.2, 0.8, 0.1]
    queen → [0.21, 0.79, 0.12]

---

## Sentence Embeddings

Instead of words, entire sentences are converted into vectors.

Example:
- "I love AI"
- "I enjoy machine learning"

These get similar vectors.

---

## Graph Embeddings

Graph embeddings represent nodes in a graph as vectors.

Used in:
- Social networks
- Recommendation systems
- Fraud detection

---

## Example Use Case (Social Network)

- Users = nodes  
- Friendships = edges  
- Embeddings help recommend friends

---

## Node2Vec Idea

Learns node representations using random walks on graphs.

---

## Applications of Embeddings

- Search engines
- Chatbots
- Recommendation systems
- Fraud detection
- Image recognition

---

## Python Example (Word Vectors Concept)

    from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import CountVectorizer

    text = ["I love AI", "AI is powerful"]

    vectorizer = CountVectorizer()
    X = vectorizer.fit_transform(text)

    print(X.toarray())

---

## Summary
- Embeddings convert text/graphs into numbers
- Similar items have similar vectors
- Used in NLP and graph analysis
- Word2Vec and Node2Vec are popular methods
