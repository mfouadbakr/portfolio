# 3. Statistical Foundations

Statistics is the backbone of data mining and machine learning. It helps us understand, summarize, and interpret data.

---

## Types of Data

- **Numerical Data** → Age, salary, marks  
- **Categorical Data** → Gender, colors, labels  

---

## Measures of Central Tendency

### Mean (Average)

    mean = (sum of values) / (number of values)

Example:

    data = [10, 20, 30, 40]

    mean = sum(data) / len(data)

---

### Median

Middle value after sorting data.

    data = [10, 20, 30, 40, 50]
    median = 30

---

### Mode

Most frequently occurring value.

    data = [1, 2, 2, 3, 4]
    mode = 2

---

## Measures of Spread

### Variance

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Variance shows how much data spreads around the mean.

---

### Standard Deviation

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Standard deviation measures average distance from mean.

---

## Probability Basics

Probability measures the chance of an event.

    P(event) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes

Example:

    Probability of rolling a 3 on a dice = 1/6

---

## Types of Probability

- Theoretical Probability  
- Experimental Probability  
- Conditional Probability  

---

## Correlation

Correlation shows relationship between variables.

- Positive → both increase together  
- Negative → one increases, other decreases  
- Zero → no relation  

---

## Python Example

    import numpy as np

    data = [10, 20, 30, 40, 50]

    print(np.mean(data))
    print(np.std(data))
    print(np.var(data))

---

## Summary
- Mean, median, mode describe center
- Variance & std show spread
- Probability handles uncertainty
- Correlation shows relationships
